# Funnel Analysis: Coursera

**Category:** EdTech / Subscription Platform
**Analysis Date:** April 2026

---

## 1. Jobs-to-be-Done

| User | Job |
|---|---|
| **Buyer (Learner)** | I want to grow my professional skills |
| **Seller (Institution)** | I want a single platform that gives my courses global reach |

---

## 2. Awareness

How users discover Coursera:

- **Google Search** — user actively searches for a course or skill
- **Facebook Ads** — paid social targeting career-focused segments

---

## 3. Consideration

Steps between discovery and commitment:

1. User visits the website and browses courses
2. Signs up for free — no payment required at this stage
3. Enrolls in a course for free (audit access)
4. Offered a 7-day free trial for full certificate access

---

## 4. Funnel Leak

**Primary leak: Credit card required before the trial begins**

User is asked for payment commitment before experiencing the product. This creates friction at the exact moment the user is still deciding — and many drop off here.

---

## 5. Remarketing

Coursera's recovery strategy for users who dropped at the leak:

- **Email marketing** with a discount code to address price objection
- **Email newsletter** surfacing available courses to keep the platform top of mind

---

## 6. Conversion

User receives discount email → clicks the link → purchases a reduced-cost subscription.
